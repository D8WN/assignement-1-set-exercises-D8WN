| Mango Sticky Rice

In my country, we love tropical desserts from multiple neighbouring countries.

For now, we are introducing a delicious dessert from Thailand. Mango & Sticky Rice is a popular Thai dessert made with glutinous rice steamed with coconut milk and served with ripe mangoes. It is believed to have originated in the central region of Thailand and is commonly served as a snack or dessert.

Ingredients

Coconut Rice

  • 1 Cup Dry Glutinous Rice
  • 1 Cup Full Fat Coconut Milk
  • 3-4 Tbsp Granulated Sugar
  • 1/2 Tsp Sea Salt

Ube

  • 3-4 Tbsp Halaya or Ube Spread
  • 2-3 Drops of Ube Extract (Optional)

Coconut Sauce

  • 1/2 Cup Full Fat Coconut Milk
  • 2-3 Tbsp Granulated Sugar

Corn Starch Slurry

  • 1 Tsp Corn Starch
  • 2 Tsp room temperature water

Mangoes

  • 2 Large Ripe Mangoes
  • Toasted Sesame Seeds (For Topping)

Cooking Methods

Preparing and Soaking the Sticky Rice

Wash the rice 3–4 times until the water is almost clear. Soak overnight for 8–24 hours, then drain.

Steaming the Rice

Steam the soaked rice on a lined bamboo basket for 20–25 minutes until cooked and chewy.

Cooking the Sticky Rice
  1. Heat coconut milk, sugar, and salt in a pan.
  2. Boil, reduce heat, stir until sugar dissolves.
  3. Mix in rice and cook until thickened (4 mins).
  4. Let cool for 5 minutes.
Coconut Sauce
  1. Heat coconut milk and sugar in a pan.
  2. Boil and lower heat.
  3. Add cornstarch slurry, stir until thick.

Assembling your Mango Sticky Rice

  1. Peel and slice the mangoes into cheeks and then strips.
  2. Plate rice and mangoes, sprinkle with sesame seeds, and drizzle coconut sauce. Best enjoyed fresh!
